The Store


At an early age

My eyes peering

Over the counter top

Larry and I

Worked Christmas time

In the store

Larry manned the register

He was good at math

No fingers

I held the door

"Welcome to Scheinfeld's"

"Merry Christmas"

I see Daddy

High on the ladder

A tower of boxes balanced

In on hand

A magician

On a wire

I never saw him

Drop or stumble

Impossible

He was the master

With power and grace

Beyond this place

I hear the music

Playing Christmas

Unheard at home

Known then by heart

"I ayaway!" Daddy shouted

I dashed with the ticket

Through the coats

Down the stairs

To the burried basement

Sifting through boxes

Filmed in dust

I found it

Wiped it clean

Sometimes I hid

Down there, Shh...

And slept on the boxes

Tired of the jumble

Peaceful and quiet

Eyes closed

Then the bang of the

broom

It's busy

Daddy's sign

I dashed up

Popped out

Through the coats

And into the light
